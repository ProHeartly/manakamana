The Product

What is Duna Tapari?

A centuries-old Nepali tradition, now more relevant than ever.

01

Tapari

A flat, circular plate pressed from Sal (Shorea robusta) leaves. Used across Nepal for serving food at religious ceremonies, weddings, and community feasts. Sturdy, natural, fully compostable.

02

Duna

A small bowl-shaped vessel made from the same Sal leaves, used for serving curries, sweets, and prasad during pujas and festivals. The perfect companion to the Tapari.

03

Sal Leaf

Sourced from Nepal's Terai forests. Naturally strong, water-resistant, and food-safe — no chemicals or coatings needed. After use, they return to the earth within weeks.

How It's Made

From Forest to Your Table

A simple, honest process — no machines, no chemicals, no shortcuts.

01

Leaf Collection

Fresh Sal leaves carefully collected from sustainable forest sources in Nepal's Terai region.

02

Cleaning & Drying

Leaves are washed with clean water and sun-dried to the right moisture level for pressing.

03

Pressing & Shaping

Leaves are layered and pressed using traditional molds to form the Tapari or Duna shape.

04

Quality Check

Every batch is inspected for shape, strength, and cleanliness before being packed.

05

Packed & Delivered

Orders are packed in bundles and dispatched to customers across Nepal and beyond.
